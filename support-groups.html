Support Groups

Find Connection and Understanding

Support groups are structured gatherings where individuals with similar experiences or challenges come together to share their stories, offer mutual support, and learn from one another. These groups can be particularly beneficial for people dealing with mental health conditions, chronic illnesses, or other life challenges (National Institute of Mental Health, 2021).

Available Groups:

  • Anxiety and Panic Disorder Support
  • Depression Recovery Group
  • Grief and Loss Support
  • PTSD and Trauma Recovery
  • Addiction Recovery Support
  • Caregiver Support Group

How Groups Work:

Support groups meet regularly, either weekly or bi-weekly, and are available both online and in-person. Each session is facilitated by a trained mental health professional who ensures a safe and supportive environment. Groups typically last 60-90 minutes and follow a structured format that encourages sharing and mutual support.

Benefits of Group Support:

  • Reduce feelings of isolation and loneliness
  • Learn from others' experiences and coping strategies
  • Build meaningful connections and friendships
  • Gain different perspectives on your challenges
  • Feel understood and validated
